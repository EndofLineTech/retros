## 3. What the PO Could Improve

At turn 11 the PO said "ship the fix" with no qualifier, immediately after `z7bfj` completed but with the three prep docs (ADR-007, threat model, STRIDE addenda) still uncommitted in the working tree. I had to infer "the fix" meant `z7bfj` only — based on the earlier "kick off z7bfj" choice over "ship the docs" — and shipped a test-only PR while leaving the docs in the tree, then asked at turn 12 whether to ship those too. That worked out, but it cost a turn and the inference could have gone the other way (a more aggressive read of "ship the fix" would have bundled everything into one PR, which would have been wrong here because the docs hadn't been re-confirmed).

Concrete improvement: when there are multiple distinct in-flight changesets, "ship the fix" is ambiguous in a way that "ship z7bfj" or "ship z7bfj + the docs" is not. The current shipping convention works perfectly when there's one obvious thing to ship; it gets one inference-turn slower when there are two or three. A one-word qualifier ("ship z7bfj", "ship the docs", "ship everything") would remove the inference. Small thing, repeated across sessions.

## 5. What Would Make the Project Better

A first-class "release-cut marker" bead pattern would clean up cross-epic serialization. To enforce the PO's "DBAS doesn't happen until 0.17.0 ships" decision, I added cross-epic dependencies (each `0i2vt.N` Phase-0/1 bead now `depends on project-d-skqln`). That works mechanically — they fall out of `bd ready` until the Stats v2 epic closes — but it's architecturally muddy: a v0.18.0 DBAS bead has no real reason to depend on a v0.17.0 Stats v2 bead, and anyone reading the dep graph six months from now will wonder why. A "v0.17.0 cut shipped" milestone bead that closes at release-cut time, with serializing-children depending on *it*, would be cleaner and reusable for the next serialization decision (and for the same pattern in `tp1pd`/`7i2vv` where I also tangled an "in this epic" intent with a "blocked by" mechanism, requiring a later cleanup).

This is small but recurring — every multi-epic project hits it. Worth a `bd` convention or a project ADR.

## 7. Lessons for Future Sessions

- **Keep**: Independent parent-side gate verification before shipping subagent work, per CLAUDE.md global guidance. Re-running the test suite myself after the `skqln.2` agent finished caught nothing this time, but the discipline is what makes it trustworthy when it does catch something. The two-phase standup pattern (Phase 1 triage → Phase 2 deep-dive only for non-greens) also kept costs down without losing signal.
- **Stop**: Filing follow-up beads mid-grooming when a persona notes a sibling concern. The bead system should be a backlog, not a notepad. Surface findings as notes; let the PO decide whether they earn a bead.
- **Stop**: Presenting grooming candidates without pre-scoring them by customer value. "Small + quick to make ready" is a process bias, not a value frame. The grooming skill's "Step 1.5: User Value Gate" is meant to happen *before* persona-spawning, not as a post-hoc PO veto.
- **Start**: Sanity-checking the grooming candidate list against the live codebase before spawning personas. `git log -- <file>` / `git grep <pattern>` for any bead claiming a specific source-code fix is a 30-second check that would have killed `681a1` from the candidate list before 10 agents evaluated it.
- **Start**: Framing standup-domain YELLOWs in customer-value terms, not delivery-velocity terms. "Work scoped but not flowing" is a PM-domain frame; "Stats v2 critical path has no movement" is a customer-value frame. The same finding, two framings; the second is the one that gives the PO a direct decision.
- **Value learning**: The customer doesn't care about ADR-005 monthly-audit cadences, runbook style consistency, or speculative test sweeps. They care about features (the Users panel) and stability (their upgrade not failing). Every other category needs to justify itself by harm-reduction or feature-advancement, not by domain hygiene. The PO's pushback at turn 15 was sharper than my own value gate — and the gap is fixable, not a personality difference.
